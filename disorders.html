Stress-Related Disorders in International Students

Physical Disorders

1. Sleep Disorders (Insomnia): Chronic stress interferes with sleep onset and maintenance. International students often experience irregular sleep cycles due to anxiety about academic performance and time zone/ schedule changes, leading to fatigue and impaired cognitive function.

2. Gastrointestinal Issues: Stress affects the gut-brain axis, causing symptoms like stomach aches, nausea, and irritable bowel-like complaints. Dietary changes and anxiety exacerbate these physical manifestations.

Psychological Disorders

1. Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Persistent excessive worry about academic success, cultural adaptation, and future prospects can evolve into GAD, characterized by restlessness, difficulty concentrating, and irritability.

2. Major Depressive Disorder (MDD): Feelings of hopelessness, withdrawal, low mood, and loss of interest can stem from prolonged isolation, perceived failures, and chronic stress without support.
