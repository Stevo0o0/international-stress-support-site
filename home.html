About / Personal Story

When I arrived in Canada, the excitement came with pressure: new systems, financial uncertainty, and quiet loneliness. This site shares what I’ve learned — research-backed techniques and real supports that help me manage stress and keep moving forward.

Understanding Stress & Why International Students

What is stress? Stress is the body's response to perceived challenges or demands that exceed one’s coping resources. It can manifest physically, emotionally, and cognitively, especially when multiple pressures accumulate.

Why I chose international students: As someone who came to a new country for education, I experienced cultural adjustment, academic pressure, and isolation. International students often navigate these while far from familiar support networks, making stress particularly pronounced.

How stress affects me as an international student: I’ve felt sleep disruption, constant worry about academic performance, and a sense of not belonging, which lowered motivation and increased fatigue. These experiences mirror what many others face.

Top Sources of Stress for International Students

  • Acculturative stress: Adapting to new norms and identity tension. [1]
  • Social isolation: Lacking belonging in unfamiliar environments. [2]
  • Academic pressure: New educational expectations and performance anxiety. [3]
  • Financial strain: Tuition, cost of living, and currency concerns. [4]
  • Stigma around seeking help: Cultural and personal barriers to mental health support. [5]
