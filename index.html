About / Personal Story

When I arrived in Canada, the excitement came with pressure: new systems, financial uncertainty, and quiet loneliness. This site shares what I’ve learned — research-backed techniques and real supports that help me manage stress and keep moving forward.

Why International Students?

  • Acculturative stress: Adapting to new norms and identity tension. [1]
  • Social isolation: Lacking belonging in unfamiliar environments. [2]
  • Academic pressure: New educational expectations and performance anxiety. [3]
  • Financial strain: Tuition, cost of living, and currency concerns. [4]
  • Stigma around seeking help: Cultural and personal barriers to mental health support. [5]

Impacts of Stress

Physical

Sleep disruption, headaches, gastrointestinal symptoms—common somatic expressions when stress goes unmanaged. [6]

Psychological

Anxiety, depression, rumination, and reduced academic performance are frequent. [7]

Tips & Techniques

Quick Tips

  • Build peer support with regular check-ins. [8]
  • Maintain a structured routine (sleep, study, breaks). [9]
  • Normalize help-seeking via campus services. [10]
  • Plan and budget finances to reduce uncertainty. [11]
  • Engage in cultural identity–affirming activities. [5]

Deep Dive: Mindfulness

Practice a 3–5 minute focused-breathing exercise: sit comfortably, breathe in slowly for 4 counts, exhale for 4, notice thoughts without judgment, gently return to breath. Evidence shows mindfulness reduces anxiety and stress. [12]

Deep Dive: Cognitive Reframing

Identify negative automatic thoughts (e.g., “I will fail”), challenge them (“One mistake doesn’t define me”), and replace with balanced alternatives. Combine with small behavioral activations like a short walk. [13]

Deep Dive: Social Support

Schedule weekly buddy check-ins using prompts: “What was hard this week?” and “What went well?” Social connection buffers stress. [8]
